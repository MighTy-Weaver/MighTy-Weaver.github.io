E-Commerce Behavior Assertion Plausibility and Typicality

Hi! Welcome to this experimental HITs. In this survey, your task in to rate two sentences' plausibility and typicality. These two metrics will be explained later.
But first, please take a look at what's an Invalid sentence. If a sentence is INVALID, you are required to choose INVALID for BOTH questions instead of rating them by two metrics.

Invalid Assertion

Here we provide some examples for you to understand what makes an invalid assertion:

  • Incomplete assertion: PersonX bought a steak and a bacon because they are all
  • Garbled assertion: PersonX bought a television and a phone because they are derived from 2 the early 1960's, 70's to the present, the '80s, '90s etc, of course.."
  • Unrelated assertion: PersonX bought a Halloween costume and Halloween candies because they can be used to play soccer.
  • Assertion only related to one item: PersonX bought a battery and a mask because they are symbols of energy.
  • Incorrect contents: PersonX bought a pair of shoes and a pair of sneakers because they are made of plastic.
  • Useless/Repeated information: PersonX bought a McDonald's Big Mac because it has the property of being a "McDonald's Big Mac".

VERY IMPORTANT: if you think a sentence is invalid, please choose INVALID for both questions. Otherwise, it may cause a rejection!

Plausibility

For question 1 and 3, you will be asked to answer whether the sentence make sense or not. Here, it means that whether you think the sentence is a plausible reason for the occurring of such shopping behavior. In other words, is the given sentence a plausible/correct reason for PersonX to buy BOTH items?


Please note that: If you think the given sentence is VERY UNLIKELY to happen (nobody would do online shopping for such reason), please choose No, not plausible. There isn't a specific rule for defining how unlikely it is, please use your own judgement on this. Generally, most of the sentences should be plausible, but there should still a few that is not plausible.

Typicality

Then, you will be asked about the typicality of the given sentences at question 2 and 4. Typicality indicates whether the sentence presented is a typical reason for buying the two products. Specifically, how well does the sentence reflects the items' properties, functions, intentions, usages that cause PersonX buy both items?
You will be required for rating each sentence with 3 choices: Very typical, somewhat typical, and not typical. You can understand them as given them a full mark, half mark or zero mark. More examples are provided below for your understanding:


For example, when we buy two costumes, VERY typical sentences may be:

  • Because they can make PersonX looks like characters in the opera.
  • Because they can make PersonX beautiful.
  • Because they are light and easy to wear.
  • Because they are characters in the ...... Opera.
  • Because PersonX likes to wear them in the play.
  • Because they are related to characters in the play.

SOMEWHAT typical sentences may be:

  • Because they are made of good materials.
  • Because they both are defined as costumes.
  • Because they both are symbol of costumes.
  • because they both are the best products in their category.

NOT typical sentences may be:

  • Because PersonX likes them.
  • Because they both have a property of clothes (or items are clothes)
  • Because they are clothes
  • Because they both are products.
  • Because they can be used by PersonX.

Please also notice that, there are many sentences claiming that the two items are from the same company, same manufacturer, same brand, same product line, etc. Please pay special care on these sentences, if the two items are indeed from the same ____ as claimed in the sentence, then please choose PLAUSIBLE AND SOMEWHAT TYPICAL (0.5), otherwise, it should be INVALID.

Some examples of this kind of sentences:

  • they are both used for the same purpose.
  • they are created by the same company.
  • they are created by the same manufacturer.
  • they are derived from the same product line.
  • they both are a part of the same series.
  • they are derived from the same source.
  • they are derived from the same manufacturer.
  • they both are a part of the same promotion.
  • they both are a manner of the same brand.
  • they both are a part of the same product line.
  • they are derived from the same product family.
  • they could both be used for the same purpose.
