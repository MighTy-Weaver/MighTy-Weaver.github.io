But first, please take a look at what's an Invalid sentence. If a sentence is INVALID, you are required to choose INVALID (the last choice) instead of rating them.

Invalid Assertion

Here we provide some examples for you to understand what makes an invalid assertion:

  • Incomplete assertion: PersonX bought a steak and a bacon because they are all
  • Garbled assertion: PersonX bought a television and a phone because they are derived from 2 the early 1960's, 70's to the present, the '80s, '90s etc, of course.."
  • Unrelated assertion: PersonX bought a Halloween costume and Halloween candies because they can be used to play soccer.
  • Assertion only related to one item: PersonX bought a battery and a mask because they are symbols of energy.
  • Incorrect information: PersonX bought a pair of shoes and a pair of sneakers because they are made of plastic.
  • Useless/Repeated information: PersonX bought a McDonald's Big Mac because it has the property of being a "McDonald's Big Mac".
    Note that repeating any item's name in the sentence will make the sentence INVALID.

VERY IMPORTANT: if you think a sentence is invalid, please choose INVALID instead of other three options.



Is the QUALITY of this sentence acceptable?

You'll find out that each sentence describes a shopping behavior of PersonX. They describe what causes / why PersonX bought two items. Your task is to decide whether the quality of given sentence is acceptable or not. Specifically, you should consider it in the way that how well does the sentence reflect / characterize the items' properties, functions, intentions, and usages, that might trigger / cause PersonX buy both of them?

The best sentence should satisfy these two requirements:
(1) The sentence contains rich information containing the items' detailed properties, functions, intentions, or usages. (one of them is enough if sufficiently detailed)
(2) The sentence presented a reasonable reason for PersonX's to buy these two items.


You'll be provided with three rating options: Strongly Accept, Weakly Accept, and Reject.

Informativeness Casuality Examples Explanations
Strongly Accept The sentence contains detailed information regarding the two items. It contains the properties, or functions, or intentions, or usages of two items. The information about the two items in this sentence is an important reason for PersonX to buy them. PersonX bought Two Casio Watches:
(1) Because they are capable of telling time.
(2) Because they are expensive luxury watches.
(3) Because they are made of silver and high quality material.
(4) Because they are comforatble and easy to wear. 		(1) Describe the functions/usages of Casio watches: Telling time. A strong reason for shopping.
(2) Describe the property of Casio watches: luxury items. Strong reason for shopping.
(3) Describe the properties of two Casio watches: silver and high quality material. Strong reason for shopping.
(4) Describe the usages/properties of Casio watches: comfortable and easy to wear. Strong reason for shopping.
Weakly Accept The sentence contains limited information regarding the two items. The information is not reflecting / characterizing the items specific features. The information in this sentence about the two items does not form an important part of PersonX's reason for buying them. PersonX bought Two Casio Watches:
(1) Because they are both watches.
(2) Because they are used for the same purpose.
(3) Because they are made by the same company.
(4) Because they are gifts for his wife. 		(1) Limited information, both watches can be any watches, not a detailed information for buying two Casio watches.
(2) Though they are both used for the same purpose, the sentence didn't claim what purpose it is, so the information is not detailed enough.
(3) Not a good reason, if the sentence claims made by Casio, then it's a good sentence. However, "same company" is too broad, other two watches with the same brand can also satisfy, not necesarrily has to be Casio watches.
(4) Other items such as rings can also be gifts for his wife, it is not the primary purpose of two watches. Thus it doesn't form a strong reason for buying two items.
Reject The sentence contains no information regarding the two items. Or the information is too general such that any other items would also work for the sentence. The information about the two items in this sentence does not reflect why PersonX specifically bought those items. In other words, it does not reflect what characteristics of the commodity lead to the purchase behavior. PersonX bought Two Casio Watches:
(1) Because PersonX likes them.
(2) Because they are distinct from each other.
(3) Because they are similar to the items he bought before. 		(1) Too general, we can know nothing about the two items. Though this is a very good reason for shopping, it has poor informativeness.
(2) Dinstinct from each other is true, but it is purely useless information. Of course two items are different. No useful information.
(3) Same as (2), purely useless information.
